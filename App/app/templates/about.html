What is the graph meant to tell us?

The graph calculates five different retirment plans and their respective annual withdrawals based on the selected return stream. The three main lines of interest are the purple, green, and red lines. Respectively these lines represent the maximum, average, and minimum additonal contribution by percent of salary to make the employee's average annual withdrawal equal to or slightly greater than the annual value of the calculated pension plan. The additonal contribution percentages are calculated by finding the minimum additional contribution required to make average annual withdrawal equal to or greater than the annual value of the pension plan for each 35 year return period from 1928-2021, for each given portfolio weight (e.q. 50% equity, 50% bonds). After calculating contribtution percent for all possible periods for a given portfolio weight the percentages are averaged. Once all portfolio weights have a corresponding average additional contribution percent to equate the base 401(k) to the calculated pension plan, the maximum value among them is selected along with the minimum value. Also, the contribution percents are averaged to find the average employer contribution percent required to equate the base 401(k) to the calculated pension plan independent of portfolio weight. Finally, with these three values, three new 401(k)s are calculated and graphed.
